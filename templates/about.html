About

Karanvir Singh Prihar

Energy Data Analyst

Mechanical and Aerospace Engineer and Registered Energy Advisor transitioning into data analytics. Two years in automotive manufacturing, several years auditing homes across Canada, and a growing conviction that the energy sector needs better data thinking.

The long way into data

My path started with a mechanical engineering degree from IIT Ropar in India, followed by two years in automotive manufacturing at Tata Motors.

I then completed a master's in aerospace engineering at the University of Toronto. When engineering work in Canada didn't pan out the way I'd hoped, I spent time in different roles before becoming a certified Registered Energy Advisor — walking through hundreds of homes with a blower door, a thermal camera, and a clipboard full of measurements.

Every audit ended with a spreadsheet: air leakage rates, heat loss coefficients, estimated savings by upgrade path. I was doing data work long before I called it that.

Now I'm making that transition explicit — learning Python, SQL, and statistical analysis, and applying data thinking to the energy sector I already understand deeply.

My edge isn't just technical skill. It's knowing what the numbers mean before the model runs, and knowing which questions are worth asking in the first place.
